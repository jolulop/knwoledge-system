# Private equity and M&A integration

Private equity firms are leaning more heavily on mergers and acquisitions to build
value when entry multiples are lower and holding periods stretch out. Common plays
include rolling up several small companies to gain scale, merging two similarly sized
businesses to wring out efficiency, and combining firms whose capabilities complement
each other for a strategic edge. The payoff depends on disciplined integration —
managing the added complexity, following through on a real integration plan, and
keeping both sides aligned on goals and day-to-day operations.
